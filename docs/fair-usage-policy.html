Fair Usage & Professional Conduct Policy

Applies to all senders, recipients and operators of mail handled through maatara.io and its delegated infrastructure, including services operated by Ma'atara, prbl.ai and RME Solutions.

Our standard. Ma'atara is a global leader in secure digital operations and traceability. Every message that leaves our systems represents the people we serve and the organisations we partner with. We hold ourselves, and those we authorise to send on our behalf, to a tier of professional conduct that is beyond reproach.

1. Purpose

This policy describes the fair, lawful and respectful use of email and messaging services provided through the maatara.io domain and any sub‑domain delegated for sending or receiving mail. It exists to protect our clients, our recipients and the integrity of the global email ecosystem on which we all depend.

2. Who this policy applies to

  • Every authenticated user with a personal or shared mailbox on maatara.io.
  • Every administrator, operator or automated agent that sends, forwards or relays mail through Ma'atara infrastructure.
  • Every contractor, partner organisation or affiliated entity (including, where applicable, prbl.ai and RME Solutions) who has been granted authority to send under our brands or domains.

3. Standards of professional conduct

You agree to communicate in a manner consistent with the values of a quality, ambitious organisation that cares deeply about its customers. Specifically you will:

  • Treat every recipient — client, prospect, supplier, regulator, colleague — with courtesy, honesty and respect.
  • Write clearly and in good faith. State who you are, who you represent, and why you are writing.
  • Honour reasonable requests promptly, including unsubscribe, correction, deletion and access requests.
  • Acknowledge mistakes openly and correct them quickly. Do not retaliate against a recipient who raises a concern.
  • Maintain confidentiality of information shared in trust, and never repurpose customer data outside its agreed use.

4. Acceptable use

You may use Ma'atara mail services for legitimate business and personal correspondence directly related to your role, including:

  • Communicating with clients, suppliers, regulators and colleagues.
  • Sending transactional notifications, service updates, invoices and contracts.
  • Sending marketing or informational mail only where the recipient has a clear, prior and demonstrable basis to receive it (consent, contract, or established business relationship), in accordance with applicable law.

5. Prohibited use

The following are strictly prohibited and may result in immediate suspension, audit review and, where appropriate, referral to authorities:

  • Unsolicited bulk mail (spam), list‑bombing, or sending to scraped, purchased or otherwise non‑consented address lists.
  • Phishing, spoofing, impersonation of any person or organisation, or any attempt to obtain credentials or payment under false pretence.
  • Distribution of malware, ransomware, exploit kits, or links intended to compromise a recipient's device or account.
  • Harassment, intimidation, threats, hate speech, discriminatory content, or sexually explicit material.
  • Fraud, deception, market manipulation, or any communication that misrepresents identity, affiliation, pricing or product.
  • Disclosure of personal, health, financial or otherwise sensitive information without lawful basis and appropriate safeguards.
  • Bypassing or attempting to bypass authentication, rate limits, audit logging, retention or any other control on the Ma'atara platform.
  • Any conduct that would breach applicable law in the sender's or recipient's jurisdiction, including the Australian Spam Act 2003, the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth), the EU GDPR, the UK GDPR, CAN‑SPAM (US), CASL (Canada), and equivalent regimes elsewhere.

6. Authentication, deliverability and traceability

All outbound mail is authenticated with SPF, DKIM and DMARC at the strictest practical settings. Recipients receive only messages that have been cryptographically signed and policy‑aligned with our domain. You will not:

  • Attempt to send mail that bypasses our outbound gateway or the platform's audit trail.
  • Configure third‑party services to send as maatara.io without prior written approval and a verified DNS record set.
  • Disable or weaken authentication, retention, content scanning or audit logging on systems under your control.

Every send is logged with sender identity, recipient set, timestamp, message hash and delivery outcome. These records are immutable and are retained in accordance with the Ma'atara records‑retention schedule.

7. Volume, fair share and reputation

Mail sending capacity is a shared resource whose value depends on the reputation of the sending domain. To preserve deliverability for everyone:

  • Sending volume must be proportionate to the legitimate business need of your role.
  • Sustained bulk sending requires written approval and a documented audience, content plan and unsubscribe mechanism.
  • Bounce, complaint and spam‑report rates must remain within industry norms (generally < 5% bounce, < 0.1% complaint). Sustained breaches will trigger automatic throttling and review.

8. Privacy and data handling

You will handle personal information in accordance with the Ma'atara Privacy Policy and all applicable data‑protection law. In particular:

  • Collect only what is necessary, use it only for the purpose for which it was provided, and retain it only as long as required.
  • Do not forward customer correspondence to personal accounts or unmanaged third‑party tools.
  • Report any suspected data incident to the security team without delay.

9. Security responsibilities

  • Use strong, unique credentials and the multi‑factor authentication provided by your identity provider (Microsoft, Google).
  • Keep your devices patched, encrypted and locked when unattended.
  • Do not share mailbox access outside the authorised assignment system. Shared mailboxes must be granted, reviewed and revoked through the admin console only.
  • Report lost devices, suspected compromise or social‑engineering attempts immediately.

10. Monitoring, audit and enforcement

Ma'atara operates a continuous audit trail across all mailboxes and administrative actions. Logs are reviewed proactively and on incident. Where a breach of this policy is identified, the response is proportionate and may include:

  • Coaching and re‑training.
  • Temporary suspension of send privileges or mailbox access.
  • Termination of access and, where appropriate, of contractual relationship.
  • Referral to law‑enforcement or regulatory bodies for unlawful conduct.

You are entitled to a fair, documented review process, and to representation, before any sanction beyond temporary suspension is applied.

11. Reporting concerns

If you receive — or are asked to send — a message that appears to breach this policy, escalate it via the in‑product report function or to the security team directly. Reports made in good faith are confidential and protected from retaliation.

12. Changes to this policy

This policy is versioned. Material changes are published through the platform and require re‑acceptance before continued use. The effective version is always the one displayed in the policy panel of your mailbox.

13. Acknowledgement

By accepting this policy you confirm that you have read, understood and will uphold the standard of conduct set out above, and that you will represent Ma'atara, prbl.ai, RME Solutions and our clients with the integrity expected of a global leader in secure digital operations and traceability.
